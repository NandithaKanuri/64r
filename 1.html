Shailaputri Devi

Shailaputri Devi is a form of the Hindu goddess Durga, worshipped on the first day of Navaratri. She is depicted as a beautiful woman riding a bull, symbolizing strength and power. Shailaputri is considered the daughter of the mountains (Shaila) and is associated with the root chakra, representing stability and grounding. Devotees seek her blessings for strength, courage, and protection from negative energies. Her worship involves chanting mantras, offering flowers, and performing rituals to invoke her divine presence.

Bramacharaini Devi

Bramacharaini Devi is a form of the Hindu goddess Durga, worshipped on the second day of Navaratri. She is depicted as a young girl holding a rosary and a water pot, symbolizing purity and devotion. Bramacharaini is associated with the sacral chakra, representing creativity and fertility. Devotees seek her blessings for self-discipline, focus, and spiritual growth. Her worship involves chanting mantras, offering fruits and flowers, and performing rituals to invoke her divine presence.

Chandraghanta Devi

Chandraghanta Devi is a form of the Hindu goddess Durga, worshipped on the third day of Navaratri. She is depicted as a fierce warrior goddess with a crescent moon on her forehead, symbolizing bravery and strength. Chandraghanta is associated with the solar plexus chakra, representing power and confidence. Devotees seek her blessings for courage, protection from evil forces, and success in their endeavors. Her worship involves chanting mantras, offering flowers and sweets, and performing rituals to invoke her divine presence.

Kushmanda Devi

Kushmanda Devi is a form of the Hindu goddess Durga, worshipped on the fourth day of Navaratri. She is depicted as a radiant goddess with a glowing complexion, symbolizing the energy of the sun. Kushmanda is associated with the sacral chakra, representing creativity and fertility. Devotees seek her blessings for health, prosperity, and the removal of obstacles. Her worship involves chanting mantras, offering fruits and sweets, and performing rituals to invoke her divine presence.

Skandamata Devi

Skandamata Devi is a form of the Hindu goddess Durga, worshipped on the fifth day of Navaratri. She is depicted as a motherly figure holding her son Skanda (Kartikeya) in her lap, symbolizing love and compassion. Skandamata is associated with the heart chakra, representing love and emotional balance. Devotees seek her blessings for maternal protection, emotional healing, and the removal of obstacles in their lives. Her worship involves chanting mantras, offering fruits and flowers, and performing rituals to invoke her divine presence.

Katyayani Devi

Katyayani Devi is a form of the Hindu goddess Durga, worshipped on the sixth day of Navaratri. She is depicted as a warrior goddess riding a lion, symbolizing strength and courage. Katyayani is associated with the solar plexus chakra, representing personal power and confidence. Devotees seek her blessings for empowerment, success in endeavors, and protection from negative energies. Her worship involves chanting mantras, offering sweets and flowers, and performing rituals to invoke her divine presence.

Kalaratri Devi

Kalaratri Devi is a fierce form of the Hindu goddess Durga, worshipped on the seventh day of Navaratri. She is depicted with a dark complexion, symbolizing the removal of darkness and ignorance. Kalaratri is associated with the root chakra, representing stability and grounding. Devotees seek her blessings for protection from fears, obstacles, and negative influences. Her worship involves chanting mantras, offering black sesame seeds and jaggery, and performing rituals to invoke her divine presence.

Mahagauri Devi

Mahagauri Devi is a serene form of the Hindu goddess Durga, worshipped on the eighth day of Navaratri. She is depicted with a fair complexion, symbolizing purity and wisdom. Mahagauri is associated with the crown chakra, representing spiritual enlightenment and connection to the divine. Devotees seek her blessings for inner peace, harmony, and the removal of obstacles on their spiritual path. Her worship involves chanting mantras, offering white flowers and fruits, and performing rituals to invoke her divine presence.

Siddhidatri Devi

Siddhidatri Devi is the ninth form of the Hindu goddess Durga, worshipped on the ninth day of Navaratri. She is depicted with four arms, holding a trident, a mace, a lotus, and a book, symbolizing knowledge and power. Siddhidatri is associated with the root chakra, representing stability and grounding. Devotees seek her blessings for the attainment of Siddhis (spiritual powers) and the removal of obstacles. Her worship involves chanting mantras, offering red flowers and fruits, and performing rituals to invoke her divine presence.
